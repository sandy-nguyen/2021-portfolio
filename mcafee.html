Designing for someone’s grandma

As I mentioned before, the tiger team’s main objective is to increase download and installation rates for cart. The problem at hand was that there was a significant amount of customers (18-20%) that purchased our product but don’t download/install it or don’t realize they can install it on other devices. We discussed the need for further UX research and created what we call a “Blue Sky Solution,” which is an overhaul of the current cart confirmation page that would guide users throughout each step of the download and installation process. This idea was proposed with our target demographic in mind (55+ aged individuals with novice levels of tech savviness).

This project is definitely one of my favorites. McAfee is a cybersecurity software company that specializes in antivirus. You might recognize the company from the infamous founder, John McAfee. For my internship, I was put on the tiger team which was in charge of increasing download and installation rates of the product. I was tasked with creating a long-term solution to decreasing installation rates.

The prototype saw a 22% increase in users who could successfully install the product and an 18% increase in users who could trigger a download.
Post-Purchase
Experience
1. Design

In the previous design, the CTA to download the software was below the fold. Users also saw the header “Thank you! Your purchase is complete.” and thought that their computer was automatically protected.

2. Error handling

Many users had issues with downloading the product. The most common issue is if they had incompatible third party software that they would need to remove before downloading our product. Users were unaware of how to seek for help, and ultimately abandoned the download process.

3. Guidance

The previous steps on how to download the product were not intuitive enough for our users. There was no guidance on how to download the product on other devices as well, so most users just assumed they could only protect whatever device they purchased the product on.

These pain points are very important to address because if our products are not being fully utilized, the user doesn’t find as much value in their purchase and ultimately do not repurchase the product when their subscription ends. With these takeaways in mind, I created user personas.

With our personas in mind, I started ideating a couple of different ideas on how to approach these pain points. I explored different layout options, gamification of the download process, integration of AI chat boxes, and etc. By the end of my exploration phase I had designed over 20 different ideas.

I then proceeded to make user task flows for the post-purchase user journey. This was needed because the guided download instructions would change depending on what operating system or device the user was using, and so there were multiple journeys/edge cases that needed to be considered. After stakeholder meetings and discussing more about engineering constraints, we decided to move forward with the solution that incorporated the ideas that were received best by our users and were doable from a backend perspective.

Guided post-purchase experience
Integrated help modal
1. Design

In the new design, there is an emphasis on the CTA with an animated double chevron to guide the user to their next steps as well.

2. Error handling

Users can now restart the download if they have issues or open up the help modal to get redirected to support.

3. Guidance

The new guided approach uses chevrons in the cards to allow the user to go back and forth between steps. The timeline lets the user to see where they are in the process. There is also an added loophole at the end of the journey for users to go through the same download process again for different devices.

I’m happy to share the full prototype upon request!
